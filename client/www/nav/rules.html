SPHERO

You start the game with a centered dot called 'the anchor.'

You can place dots in any of the four cardinal directions next to the anchor or any previously placed dots.

If four or more dots of the same color are next to each other in any of the four cardinal directions, they disappear.

Each dot is has a certain 'valence,' representing the minimum number of horizontal and vertical steps from the anchor to that dot. If the valence of the dot with the highest valence on the screen is greater by 4 or more than the valence of the dot with the lowest valence of the screen that is only connected to one other dot (or greater than 4, if there are any empty spots left next to the anchor), the game state becomes 'unbalanced.' Here is an example of an unbalanced state.

When this happens, the dots rotate and the game rebalances itself!

In multiplayer, the game ends when at least one of the dots on the board is touching the border. You'll need your opponents' help to reach the border--but if you have more dots than they do when the game ends, you win.

In solo mode, the goal is to experiment. Click on the anchor to change colors. Listen to the strange music emanating from the COSMIC SPHERES....
